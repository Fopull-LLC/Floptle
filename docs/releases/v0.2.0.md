# Floptle v0.2.0 — "Assemblies"

Multi-part vehicles are now a first-class engine concept: build a machine
from parts, fly it as one rigid body, take it apart in flight, break it in a
crash — on worlds that orbit, at any time-warp, by the hundreds.

Everything below is a generic, public engine feature (the solar demo is just
the first consumer).

## Compound rigid bodies (`floptle-physics`)

- A `RigidBody` hierarchy with the **assembly** flag becomes ONE 6-DOF
  compound body: per-part mass, composite center of mass and inertia
  (parallel-axis), sphere/box/capsule part shapes.
- Budgeted, sample-based contact resolution — a buried or deeply-spawned
  assembly un-buries at a sane rate instead of catapulting.
- `split()` detaches any subset of parts into a NEW live compound
  mid-flight (staging, breakage, cranes) — both halves keep simulating.
- **Anchoring**: a compound can be pinned where it stands (launch clamps,
  docking latches) — no gravity, no contacts, rides its planet's frame,
  releases from rest.

## Assemblies in the scene & Lua (`assembly.*`)

- Vessel prefabs: spawn parts under an assembly root, `assembly.rebuild`,
  and the physics compound gathers itself. Assembly roots read like bodies
  (`node.vx`, `node.up_x`, `node.grounded`) in every script pass.
- Tick-held forces at world points (`forceAt` — an off-center engine
  honestly torques the stack), pure torques, impulses, `split` with a Lua
  callback on the new vessel, `setAnchored`, `teleport` (THE way to place a
  live assembly), and a per-frame `info` mirror: mass, com, **origin** (the
  physics-fresh anchor — node transforms lag a rails carry inside
  `fixedUpdate`), vel, angVel, grounded, anchored, part ids.
- Vector arguments accept `vec3()` values and plain `{x,y,z}` tables
  everywhere, pinned by Rust-side contract tests.

## Per-part impact attribution — damage systems in ten lines

- Every contact a compound resolves is attributed to the PART that took it,
  with the normal impulse absorbed. `assembly.impacts(node)` returns the
  last tick's per-part loads (`{ part, impulse, x, y, z }`): compare
  against per-part strength and you have crashes, breakage and destruction
  — a soft landing reads as small impulses on the legs, a nose-first crash
  as one huge impulse on the nose.

## Orbital mechanics for machines

- **Compounds ride planet rails**: the dominant-frame carry extends to
  assemblies, with velocity-continuous SOI handoffs. A launchpad's baked
  static collider rides its orbiting planet too — surface structures stay
  exactly as solid as the terrain.
- **Warp coasting**: during time-warp every in-flight compound snaps to its
  own analytic Kepler conic — drift-free at 10 000×, velocity kept current
  so dropping to 1× resumes seamlessly; surface proximity cancels warp.
- **Distant-craft LOD**: compounds far from the camera leave live physics
  automatically (landed ones freeze in the carried frame, flyers coast on
  rails) and wake to full contact physics on approach. Hundreds of
  satellites, stages and rovers cost nothing.

## Engine quality-of-life

- `physics.pause(on)` / `physics.isPaused()` — skip the whole sim step
  while scripts, rails and streaming keep running (loading screens, pause
  menus). Held forces are dropped, never banked.
- In-game telegraph layer `draw.*` (lines, rings, spheres, boxes):
  player-facing linework rendered in the game view — attach markers, orbit
  conics, range rings — distinct from the editor's debug gizmo layer.
- Scale-aware triplanar sampling on scaled meshes.

## Solar demo (bundled reference project)

- Ship builder v1.2: catalogue, snap placement, free part ORIENTATION
  (yaw/pitch/roll — build sideways, rocket cars), radial side-mounts with a
  radial decoupler (side boosters), precise nudge/rotate of placed parts,
  whole-ship grab, undo, self-contained blueprints, CoM/CoT overlay,
  per-stage TWR/fuel readout.
- Full vessel flight: clamped pad launches on an orbiting world, staging
  (axial stages + lateral booster separation), pooled fuel, SAS hold
  modes, navball + instrument cluster, time-warp, the 3D map with
  trajectories and maneuver planning for any piloted craft, EVA/boarding,
  and per-part damage — smoke, explosions, wreckage, and losing the pod
  loses the ship.
