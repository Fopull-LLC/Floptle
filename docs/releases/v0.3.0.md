# Floptle v0.3.0 — "Orbits & Impacts"

Fly a craft from the map, feel every scrape and crash, watch distant vessels
coast their real orbits — and a stable sky at any distance. This release is
about **honest physics feedback** and **rendering robustness** on top of the
Assemblies foundation.

Everything below is a generic, public engine feature (the solar demo is just
the first consumer).

## Rendering — sky stability (`floptle-render`)

- **Fixed the intermittent black flicker in the sky / background.** The sky is
  painted by the raymarch pass, which cleared its target to black and relied on
  the full-screen sky fragment to repaint every background pixel. At large
  world/orbital scale a grazing ray hit an f32 edge (catastrophic cancellation
  in the atmosphere-shell intersection: `dot(c,c) - b·b` with `|c|` ~ hundreds
  of thousands), producing a NaN that resolves to 0 — a black pixel — as the
  camera moved each frame. Fixed three ways: the sky pass now **clears to the
  sky colour, never black**; the shell distance is computed as a stable
  perpendicular-rejection length instead of the cancelling difference; and the
  sky fragment **sanitizes any NaN back to the sky colour**. A fixed-pose
  headless probe never exercised the moving-camera edge, which is why it only
  ever showed up in the live windowed editor.

## Physics — honest impacts (`floptle-physics`)

- **Scrapes and glancing hits now register.** `compound_impacts()` used to drop
  every contact that carried no *normal* impulse — so a hull dragged along the
  ground (a topple, a belly-slide, a shallow graze) resolved purely
  positionally and was invisible to gameplay. Every genuine (penetrating)
  contact is now reported, carrying its honest closing speed, so a damage system
  can react to sliding contact, not just square-on slams.

## Distant craft — controllable & orbit-correct (`floptle-editor`)

- **`assembly.keepLive(node, on)`** — exempt a compound from the distant-craft
  LOD so it stays in full physics however far the active camera roams. Needed to
  fly a vessel from a pulled-back map/observation camera (its throttle, steering
  and orbital velocity stay live) instead of freezing on rails under you.
- **LOD-coasting craft report their true orbital velocity every tick.** A craft
  on the distance-LOD Kepler rails had its reported velocity zeroed while its
  position advanced analytically — so anything reading `assembly.info().vel`
  (map trajectories, HUD orbit read-outs) saw zero and drew nothing. It now
  carries the on-conic velocity, so a far, coasting vessel's orbit is drawable.

## Animation — robust binding (`floptle-editor`)

- **Fixed a rig-bind race.** An animation controller could bind on the first
  play frame *before* its rigged mesh finished importing, falling back to a
  node-skeleton binding (a static bind pose that never animates, since clips are
  keyed by bone name) and never re-binding. The animator now upgrades to the
  real skeleton the moment the mesh's rig is available.

## Also in this release (from the demo-driven engine work since v0.2.0)

- **Surface-locked shader input `objectPos`** — a scale-aware, object-local
  coordinate for shaders, so procedural surface detail (panel grids, weathering)
  stays put on the surface instead of swimming with the camera under floating
  origin.
- **Honest impact-speed crash metric** — `assembly.impacts().speed` is the
  pre-resolution closing velocity (m/s), uncapped by the depenetration budget,
  plus a motion-based fallback normal so a fast ram deep into a saturated SDF
  interior reports its true speed instead of ~0.
- **Spatial audio for the mixer** — project mixer buses (SFX reverb/compressor,
  UI dry, ambient EQ, master limiter) driving spatial voices.

See `docs/scripting.md` for the `assembly.*` API. All workspace tests, clippy,
and the demo smoke harnesses are green.
